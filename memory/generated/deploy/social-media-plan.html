Social Media Plan

How to read this plan

This is the social media execution plan for Maxxim, produced in the Deploy phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions of 27 June 2026, and the 1 July refresh that folded in the 29 June product mechanics and the live Jewell partner site. It sits directly downstream of the locked social-strategy.html and turns that framework into an operating plan: account setup, the 90-day calendar for the founding-cohort window, the production workflow Pip runs, post frameworks per pillar, the community management playbook, the organic-and-paid handshake, and the measurement cadence. The strategy decided which platforms and what to say. This plan decides who does what, on which day, to what standard, and how it is checked.

One product fact frames everything here. The Social Studio unlocks only once a Social Strategy exists, and that strategy now exists and is locked. Pip is therefore live: Pip prepares, a founder inspects, and only then does anything post. This plan is the brief Pip drafts against and the standard Clent and Raef sign against. It is also the worked example, because Maxxim runs its own social exactly the way the model says client work should be run: the machine carries the repeatable 80 percent, a named human owns the 20 percent where quality is decided.

The audience and posture are inherited, not reargued. The near-term customer is the trusted operator, the Capacity-Capped Client Partner (Operator Owen in the customer-profile.html), a B2B, founder-led, LinkedIn-weighted audience. Social is a founder-led thought-leadership channel voiced by Clent and Raef. End-client awareness rides on the same content through partner reshares, it does not get its own programme. LinkedIn is the home at roughly 80 percent of effort, YouTube is the proof vault, the owned newsletter is the nurture layer, and TikTok, a Facebook page, Threads and Pinterest stay skipped.

The same discipline as every upstream document applies. Real anchors are stated as real: the Growth Diagnostic at $4,950 credited forward, the premium band of $25,000 to $250,000 with a recent run near $80,000, the specialist module near $1,500. Every engagement benchmark, follower assumption and budget figure in this plan is illustrative, pending pilots, and requires Clent sign-off before it is treated as locked or used externally. Open items are carried, not invented: Maxxim's category lead word stays open, the front-door product stays open, partner economics stay illustrative, and the fractional CMO model is how Jewell packages the method as a partner, not a Maxxim fact. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only cleared public Maxxim proof, OTR Earthmover never appears in public content, Hidrive informs a point as a percentage only and is never named, and Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are never re-used as Maxxim's public proof. House style is locked and hard: no em dashes, no exclamation marks, Australian English, operator to operator, never hype, and the business is Jewell, never the corrupted form. Ownership is Clent and Raef only. Nothing posts externally without a founder's sign-off.

2. Account Architecture and Channel Setup

Founder profiles are the primary surface, not a brand page. Owen buys from a person he can scrutinise, so the accounts that carry the programme are Clent's and Raef's. The Maxxim company presence exists to validate, not to broadcast.

LinkedIn, priority 1

  • Clent's profile. The architect and gate-holder voice. Headline carries the method and the human position, operator to operator, for example the 35-year operator who built the gated method and now runs the engine behind it. About section tells the Jewell-to-Maxxim story in first person: the method proven over years, the cap every good operator hits, the engine that runs the repeatable 80 percent while a named human signs the 20 percent. Featured section pins the Pottsville proof, the method walkthrough video once live, and the partner page on the site. Exact headline and About wording is drafted by Max from the copy-deck.html and signed by Clent.
  • Raef's profile. The engine and platform voice. Headline carries the builder position, the person who makes the machine legible. About section explains Max, Sal and Pip as processes, the mechanical gates, and why the machine never takes credit the human is accountable for. Featured section pins the engine-shown video and the 80/20 carousel.
  • Maxxim company page. Minimal by design. Locked identity (Signal Blue #2D5BFF, Poppins, Edition 03, reversed wordmark on dark grounds), a one-paragraph description carrying the engine-behind-the-partner line, a link to the site, and reshares of founder posts only. No original content programme on the page in this window. The page exists so that a prospect who checks the company finds a coherent, on-brand surface, not so it can chase reach.
  • Creator mode and follow settings. Both founders switch profiles to follow-first with topics aligned to the pillars, so cold readers can follow without a connection request.

YouTube, priority 2

  • One Maxxim channel, branded to the locked identity, with the reversed wordmark on the dark banner and Signal Blue accents. Channel description carries the same one-paragraph position as the company page.
  • Videos are structured as durable proof assets: strong plain titles (what is shown, not clickbait), chapters at every step and gate, a pinned comment linking to the site, and end-frames in Signal Blue with the reversed wordmark.
  • Playlists mirror the pillars: The Method (walkthroughs), The Proof (Pottsville and future cleared cases), The Engine (Max, Sal and Pip at work).

Owned newsletter, priority 3

  • The monthly founder note to the warm partner list. It is operated under the edm-plan.html (Programme 3, partner community communications) and is fed by this plan, not run by it: Pip repurposes the month's best posts and proof into a draft, Max shapes the note, a founder signs. Nothing is written fresh for it.

Opportunistic and skipped

  • X / Twitter. Repost founder takes only where it costs near-zero time. Pip may mirror approved LinkedIn text posts. No native programme, no reply obligation beyond courtesy.
  • Instagram. Held for later, partner-run, end-client-facing proof once the partner motion is proven. Not opened in this window.
  • TikTok, Facebook page, Threads, Pinterest. Skipped, per the strategy. Any request to open one routes to Clent as a strategy change, not a scheduling decision.

3. The Production Workflow

The workflow is the 80/20 seam made operational. Pip is Prepare, Inspect, Post, and the Inspect step is human by rule. Three batching rituals carry the whole programme; none of them requires more than the two founders and the engine.

The monthly proof session

One recording block per month produces the YouTube asset and everything harvested from it. Record the demonstration once (a method walkthrough, a Pottsville teardown, or Max, Sal or Pip running a step with a founder signing the gate), then Pip cuts two to three short clips and Sal builds one to two carousels from the same material. Video cost is paid once and repurposed many times. The photo-video-brief.html governs the production standard; nothing here needs a crew, a clean screen recording with a founder piece-to-camera is the format.

The quarterly case harvest

Each quarterly showcase and each cleared-proof milestone is a content factory: one event yields weeks of Pillar 4 and Pillar 5 material. The September checkpoint (cohort target date, 30 September) is the first planned harvest after the 3 July founding session.

Minimum viable week

If a week collapses, the programme does not. The floor is two founder text posts, one each from Clent and Raef, drafted by Pip in the Monday session's shortest form, plus replies on whatever is live. Founder text plus carousels alone can carry every pillar; video is the first thing to pause and the last thing to let block the schedule.

5. Post Frameworks by Pillar

These are the skeletons Pip drafts from, so no slot starts blank. They are frameworks with illustrative openers, not finished copy; the copy-deck.html governs voice and every draft is founder-signed before it queues. One claim per post, sized to the proof. Emphasis comes from the claim, not punctuation.

P1, the 80/20 thesis (the Monday claim)

  • Shape: one sharp, true claim in the first line, two to four short paragraphs of operator-to-operator argument, one grounded example, a quiet close that invites the reader's experience rather than pitching.
  • Illustrative openers: "AI made the first 80 percent of this work cheap. It did not touch the 20 percent a client actually pays for." Or: "Every capable operator hits the same cap. It is not a talent problem, it is an hours problem."
  • Never: hype, futurism, or a claim bigger than the cleared proof behind it.

P2, the human moat (the trust take)

  • Shape: name the objection honestly, then answer it structurally: the gate, the named human, the sign-off. Close on accountability shown, not asserted.
  • Illustrative opener: "The real question about AI work is not whether it is good. It is whether someone will put their name on it."
  • Never: mock the sceptic. The sceptic is the customer.

P3, the engine shown (the demo)

  • Shape: show one step running (Max, Sal or Pip), end on the human decision. The machine proposes, the founder signs. Caption states plainly what was machine and what was human.
  • Illustrative opener: "This post was drafted by Pip, our social process. It published because a human read it and signed it. That order is the whole point."
  • Guardrail: Max, Sal and Pip are always features of Maxxim, subordinate to the accountable human, never the accountable party and never separate products.

P4, the proof it works (the evidence)

  • Shape: a real run, told plainly: what the client needed, what the method did at each gate, what shipped, what the client owns. Numbers only where cleared and real.
  • Illustrative opener: "Pottsville Acupuncture needed a working commercial presence, not a project. Here is what a full Discover, Design, Deploy run looks like."
  • Clearance, restated because it is the highest-risk pillar: Pottsville only in public. OTR Earthmover never. Hidrive as an unnamed percentage only, with clearance. Jewell cited as proof the partner model works, attributed to Jewell, and Jewell's named client results never re-used as Maxxim's own proof.

P5, the table (the community)

  • Shape: warmer, reciprocal, the table voice. A moment from the community, a partner win reshared with consent, an open question put to the wider network.
  • Illustrative opener: "The most useful hour of our week is the table, a group of operators comparing what actually worked."
  • Guardrails: no named partners or client specifics without consent. Christy Kilmartin's two hats, referrer and specialist, stay separate in any spotlight. Economics questions get the honest line: structure set, numbers being proven through the founding cohort.

6. Community Management Playbook

For a brand whose entire thesis is the human moat, replies are part of the proof. Pip surfaces and drafts; a named founder signs every reply, the same rule that governs posting. Reply tone is warmer than post tone, reciprocal and operator to operator, still no hype and no exclamation marks.

SurfaceResponse-time targetFirst responder
LinkedIn comments on founder postsWithin 4 business hours, while the post is live in-feedThe founder whose post it is; Pip drafts options
LinkedIn direct messagesWithin 24 business hours, sooner on partner-intent signalsThe founder addressed
YouTube commentsWithin 48 hours; pin and answer the substantive onesRaef, with Pip surfacing
Newsletter and partner hub repliesSame day where possible; this is the warm listClent or Raef by topic

Escalation triggers

  • Partner-licence intent. Any signal that an operator is seriously evaluating escalates out of the comments into a direct founder conversation within one business day, and is logged against objective two.
  • End-client or sales lead. Routed to the partner who owns that relationship, because one named partner owns each client relationship. Maxxim does not work around its partners in public threads.
  • Complaint, probity or compliance question, or reputational risk. Straight to Clent, and the pr-plan.html crisis framework governs from there. No holding reply goes out that admits, denies or speculates; acknowledge, take it to a direct channel, and escalate.
  • Open-number questions. Any question that would require quoting partner economics, fees or caps gets the honest line, structure set, numbers being proven through the founding cohort, and never an invented figure.

Proactive engagement

Fifteen to twenty minutes per founder per day: replies on own posts first, then substantive comments on the posts of operators in the target segment and in the AI-in-marketing conversation, where the honest middle, what is proven, what is open, a named human at the gate, is almost entirely unoccupied. The goal is to be a recognised, respected presence in the rooms where Owen already spends attention, not to broadcast at him.

UGC and partner amplification

Partners are encouraged to post their own wins and reshare Maxxim content; the best of it is curated into Pillar 5 with consent. A partner's public win becomes the community's proof, which is the compounding loop applied to content. Pip maintains a light consent log: what was cleared, by whom, when, for which surface.

7. The Organic and Paid Handshake

Paid is governed by the paid-media-plan.html and stays small, sharp and held back; this section only defines the handshake so the two plans never fight. The rule from the strategy holds: no paid reach before the founder voice has something proven for a cold audience to trust.

  • What may be amplified. Only posts that have already proven themselves organically, indicatively the top decile of a month's posts by qualified engagement, and only Pillar 1, 3 and 4 content. Community content (P5) is never boosted; the table is not an ad.
  • When. Not before August at the earliest, and only once the July cadence has held for four consecutive weeks. Any earlier start is Clent's call, not a scheduling decision.
  • Audience. The paid layer targets the same Capacity-Capped Operator profile the paid media plan defines. Amplification exists to put proven proof in front of more Owens, not to buy vanity reach.
  • Budget. Illustrative and inside the paid media plan's direct-response slice; no figure in this document is a commitment. Requires Clent sign-off before any spend.
  • Measurement separation. Paid and organic results are never blended in the scorecard. Objective one's engagement-rate target is measured on organic reach only.

8. Measurement and Reporting

Measurement runs on three clocks, all compiled by Pip, all read by the founders, none requiring a tool beyond the platforms' native analytics in this window. [Assumption: no social analytics platform is locked. If one is later named, this section is tailored to it; the metrics do not change.]

Weekly scorecard (Friday, one page)

  • Posts shipped against plan, and the cadence streak.
  • Per-post impressions, engagement rate, and saves, with the week's best claim named.
  • Qualified signals: partner-intent comments and DMs, profile-to-site clicks, new followers who match the Owen profile.
  • Reply SLA performance against the section 6 targets.
  • One sentence: what Monday's session should double down on.

Monthly review (aligned to the community cadence)

  • Progress against each of the four objectives' checkpoints in section 1.
  • Pillar balance actually shipped against the target mix (P1 roughly 25 percent, P2 20, P3 15, P4 25, P5 15), with drift explained rather than hidden.
  • YouTube asset performance as a consideration metric: watch time and returning viewers, not raw views.
  • Newsletter performance, read from the edm-plan.html reporting, not duplicated.
  • What the month proved, what it opened, fed into the monthly business review and the next month's arc.

Quarterly checkpoint (first at 30 September)

  • The four six-month targets, tracked at half-distance, reported honestly against the founding-cohort goal of 5 to 8 signed partners.
  • Attribution review: which content warmed which partner conversations, by name, internally only.
  • Kill-or-scale decisions: what earns more production (usually video), what gets cut, whether the opportunistic surfaces (X mirroring, Instagram) change status. Recommendations go to Clent; nothing scales itself.

What is deliberately not measured

Follower counts as a target, likes as a KPI, and posting volume for its own sake. A small account trusted by fifty of the right operators beats a large one admired by the wrong ten thousand. The metrics above are chosen because each one traces to a partner conversation or to the credibility that starts one.
