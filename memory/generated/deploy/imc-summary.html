1. Integrated Strategy Overview

The whole execution in four statements. These are assertions, not summaries.

  1. Every channel carries one message, partner-first: the proven method, run by a human, at AI speed. The engine behind the partner, not the name on the door. The website, the design collateral, paid, SEO, email, PR, events, and CRM all ladder to the same line and all close the same single objection, the trust gap: can AI hit a standard I will put my name on. The answer everywhere is the same, and it is now showable, not asserted: Max, Sal, and Pip carry the machine 80 percent, a named human signs the 20 percent at every mechanical gate, and Pottsville is the cleared proof.
  2. The partner is the front door, so the system recruits partners before it acquires clients, and the live Jewell partner proves the model holds. Maxxim-run channels (the 3 July session, the founder network, LinkedIn organic, light LinkedIn paid, the partner hub, and maxxim.ai as the proof surface) recruit a founding cohort of 5 to 8 operators. Jewell, a 35-year operator, already runs the whole model in market as the first live proof. Partners then acquire universal end clients on their own channels, equipped with the enablement kit and the $4,950 Growth Diagnostic. The Diagnostic is the join between the two stages.
  3. The $4,950 Growth Diagnostic is the one repeatable first sale, and the lead-source tag is the commercial spine of measurement. The Diagnostic is the only fully settled price, it qualifies fit, and it is credited forward to lower buyer risk. Every engagement it opens carries a locked lead-source tag (partner-sourced, Maxxim-sourced, referred, or Jewell-direct) set once, before delivery, in HubSpot, the planned CRM. That tag drives the clip and the finder's fee and is the single most load-bearing field in the whole model.
  4. The community is the moat, and the founding cohort is what makes it real. The 3 July session is run as the founding meeting of the partner community, not a demo, with a partner map as its output and a live run of Max, Sal, and Pip on a real business as the proof in the room. The community rhythm (weekly table, monthly review, quarterly showcase, annual offsite) is a standing event programme and a compounding Deepen loop, and it is the retention engine the rest of the system feeds.

2. Total Investment Summary

Maxxim's near-term motion is deliberately lean, proof-led, and partner-led, not spend-led. The Marketing Plan sets a budget shape of roughly 70 percent brand-and-community to 30 percent direct-response recruitment. No dollar total is committed at CP3. Every figure below is an illustrative share of a modest marketing budget, pending pilots and Clent sign-off, and the structure holds at any level Clent approves. The engine is built lean on purpose: only the thin layer (Max plus the engine) is built in-house, and the rest is best-tool-for-each-job and swappable, which keeps cost-to-serve low and is why the budget can be small.
