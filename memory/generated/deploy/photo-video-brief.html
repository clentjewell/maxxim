Photo and Video Brief

How to read this document

This is the production brief for Maxxim's founding visual content, produced in the Deploy phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions in discover-addendum.html, the locked brand-strategy.html, and the brand-guidelines.html. It was refreshed on 1 July to fold in the 29 June product mechanics and the live Jewell partner. It turns brand direction and channel requirements into shot lists, scene specs, talent and styling direction, technical formats, a repurposing plan, and production logistics. It is the document a photographer or videographer executes from, and it is also the brief the Design Studio works inside.

One mechanical fact shapes this whole document. In the Maxxim engine, imagery and video are gated: they do not unlock until the Photo and Video Brief exists. This brief is that gate. Once it is signed off, Sal, the designer in the Design Studio, can produce and adapt visual content against it, and the human partner signs off every asset before it ships. So this brief is not just direction for a human crew. It is the standard the machine must meet, and the guardrails Sal works inside. The tools behind Sal are chosen per job and are swappable: photoreal raster imagery routes to OpenAI GPT-image, video is produced with Google Veo animating a still, and premium hero imagery is produced manually in Midjourney. Naming the tools is not the point; the point is that the look stays operator-grade, real, and credible, never stocky and never abstract.

One frame governs everything below. The near-term customer is the partner, the Capacity-Capped Operator, and social is a founder-led thought-leadership channel voiced by Clent and Raef. So the priority visual content is, in order: founder portraits and talking-head thought-leadership video for Clent and Raef; capture of the 3 July showcase as the founding-community moment, for proof and for repurposing; and the Pottsville proof story, subject to client consent and clearance. The look fits the locked identity: Signal Blue #2D5BFF, Poppins, design Edition 03, Blue Frog removed. The posture is substance over gloss, credible, operator to operator. No hype, no glossy stock abstraction.

What is locked is stated plainly. What is open stays open. Per v2.1 and Gate 1, this brief does not invent Maxxim's category lead word, partner economics percentages, the front-door product, the fractional CMO model, the showcase promise wording, or Christy Kilmartin's role split. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only cleared public Maxxim proof and may be used only with client consent confirmed; OTR Earthmover is internal only and never appears in public content; Hidrive is percentages only. Jewell is now a live partner running the whole model in market, which is powerful pattern and evidence, but Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are not re-used as Maxxim's public proof. House style holds: no em dashes, no exclamation marks, Australian English, and the business is Jewell, never any corruption of the name. Ownership is Clent and Raef only. Named partners and named clients are not shown publicly without consent.

1. Production Overview and Objectives

The brand's single highest-value action is closing the cleared-proof gap and establishing founder authority. Visual content serves both. The objective is not a polished brand film; it is a durable library of credible, operator-grade assets that make the founders legible, make the method watchable, and make the community visible. The trust gap is closed by watching the work and watching the people accountable for it, not by reading claims. The engine that carries the work is now nameable, so where the engine appears the assets should show a legible system: Max doing the words, Sal doing the pixels, Pip running social, always subordinate to the human at the wheel.

Two production tiers run in this brief. Hero content is higher production and long shelf life: the founder portrait set, the Pottsville proof piece, the method walkthrough. Premium hero stills, where a generated frame is needed rather than a captured one, are produced manually in Midjourney to hold the quality bar. Social and documentary content is fast, authentic, and high volume: founder talking-head clips, the 3 July coverage, behind-the-table moments. The two are briefed differently and never conflated. The whole programme is scoped to be achievable by a small crew, because the brand posture is resource-realistic and the look should feel real, not expensive.

2. Creative Direction and Visual Style

The imagery style is set in the Brand Strategy and Brand Guidelines and is not re-opened here: real over illustrated, candid over staged, outcome over abstract. The brand sells finished, owned outcomes and accountable humans, so the camera and the generator both show real people and real launched work, never glowing-brain AI abstraction or circuit-board stock. Where the engine appears, show the structured output and the gate, not a mystery box. This holds equally for a captured photograph, a GPT-image frame, and a Veo clip: the same keyword set governs all three, so the library reads as one brand whatever the source.

Design Edition 03 is the reference for graphic treatment over footage: lower-thirds, claim cards, and end frames use Poppins and Signal Blue, weight and hierarchy for emphasis, never decoration. On dark or Signal Blue grounds, the reversed white Maxxim wordmark and white type are the correct overlay, held on a solid holding shape where the field is busy; on light grounds, the primary Signal Blue mark and Ink type, again on a holding shape where the field is busy. Because external style strips out exclamation marks, on-frame emphasis comes from the strength of the claim and the typographic scale, not punctuation. With Blue Frog removed, the system is clean and typographic; keep on-frame graphics minimal and confident, and never reintroduce a mascot of any kind.

3. Shot List Structure and Content Map

Content is organised into four photo categories and three video concepts, each mapped to a messaging pillar and a channel. The map below sets the structure before the detailed lists; it doubles as the capture checklist for the crew and the brief Sal produces against inside the Design Studio.

Pillars refer to the three messaging pillars in the Brand Strategy: Pillar 1 the engine for your judgement (80/20), Pillar 2 the human at every gate (the trust gap), Pillar 3 the community is the moat. Priority order reflects what drives the channel and what cannot be repeated. The founder portrait and talking-head set is priority one because it is the PR and social engine. The 3 July showcase is priority two because it is a single, unrepeatable date. The Pottsville story is priority three and is conditional: nothing in P2 or V2 proceeds, and nothing is published, until Pottsville's owner has given written consent and clearance is confirmed.

5. Video Concepts and Treatments

Per the locked rule, each concept fixes duration, format, and platform before creative direction. All three are designed so one capture feeds many cuts. House style applies on screen too: no exclamation marks, claims carry their own weight. Where a still can carry a moment, Veo can animate it into short motion, so a strong P4 or portrait frame can become a clip without a separate shoot.

Concept V1: Founder talking-head, the thought-leadership engine (priority 1)

  • Duration and format: captured long, cut short. Each session yields one 60 to 90 second anchor piece per topic plus several 20 to 40 second vertical cuts.
  • Platform: LinkedIn native (primary), website, partner hub, email. 16:9 master, 9:16 and 1:1 cuts.
  • Subjects: Clent and Raef, piece-to-camera, one claim per piece.
  • Treatment: single subject, eye-level, soft natural light, simple workspace or neutral background with clear space for a Poppins lower-third. Direct address, calm and plain, operator to operator. Signal Blue lower-third names the founder and the claim, with the reversed white mark where the plate is blue. No music bed louder than the voice; substance over production.
  • Content source: the three messaging pillars. Batch a month of topics in one session. Topics are drawn from the 80/20 thesis, the human moat, Pottsville and the method, and the table. The live Jewell partner is available as a talking point that the model works in market, cited as Jewell's, not folded into Maxxim's public proof.
  • Repurposing: one anchor cut for LinkedIn, two to three short vertical cuts, audio pulled for a quote-card, transcript repurposed to a text post. One sitting, many assets, drafted for social by Pip on approval.

Concept V2: Pottsville proof story (priority 3, consent-gated)

  • Duration and format: one 3 to 5 minute YouTube proof piece (the durable sales asset) plus a 60 second cut and 9:16 highlights.
  • Platform: YouTube (primary, evergreen), Work page embed, LinkedIn clip. 16:9 master, vertical highlights.
  • Subjects: the launched, owned site; the accountable human; the business owner only with signed release.
  • Treatment: a quiet, credible teardown. Show the full Discover, Design, Deploy arc through real artefacts, the launched site, and the outcome the client owns outright, hosted on their own domain. Documentary, evidence-led, no hype. A founder voiceover or to-camera frames the method; the proof persuades.
  • Hard condition: does not enter production or publication without confirmed Pottsville consent and clearance. If consent is unavailable, V2 reduces to a screen-capture walkthrough of the public site with no owner likeness and no private specifics.
  • Repurposing: the long piece anchors the Work page; a 60 second cut for LinkedIn; vertical highlights; stills pulled for the case study; quotes (cleared) for proof cards.

Concept V3: Method walkthrough, watch the engine work (priority 2)

  • Duration and format: one 8 to 12 minute YouTube walkthrough plus short clips of the engine running the spine and a gate sign-off.
  • Platform: YouTube (primary), How It Works embed, LinkedIn clips. 16:9 master, vertical and square clips.
  • Subjects: screen capture of the structured output, a founder narrating, a human signing a gate.
  • Treatment: calm, instructional, transparent. Walk the gated flow, show Max running the words, Sal the pixels, and Pip social across the repeatable 80 percent, then a named human signing the 20 percent. Show the output and the gate, never a black box. Show, concretely, that a brief like this one is what unlocks the next stage of creative in the Design Studio. Chapters and a strong title so it works as on-demand proof linked from anywhere.
  • Repurposing: the full walkthrough is an evergreen sales asset; per-gate clips seed Pillar 1 and Pillar 2; a 90 second 80/20 explainer cut; screen stills for carousels.

8. Content Repurposing Plan

The repurposing plan is not an afterthought; it determines what is captured on the day. Every hero asset is planned as one capture feeding many cuts across the channel map in the Social Strategy. The flow below shows the spine: founder session and showcase capture in, a month of assets out. Pip drafts and, on approval, publishes the social cuts, so the plan feeds a real production line rather than a folder.

Capture discipline driven by this plan: always shoot vertical-safe so 9:16 cuts exist without a reshoot; always leave clear space for Poppins type and the mark; always capture more candid texture than you think you need, because stills between claims are the cheapest, most-used assets; and always capture clean audio, because audio-only quote cards and captioned cuts multiply one talking-head into many posts. The showcase is a recurring case-study factory; one capture day produces weeks of content, and once the library exists Sal and Pip can adapt it into new formats without a new shoot.
