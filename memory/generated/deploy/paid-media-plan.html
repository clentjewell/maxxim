Paid Media Plan

How to read this plan

This is the paid advertising plan for Maxxim, produced in the Deploy phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions of 27 June 2026, and the 1 July refresh that folded in the 29 June product mechanics and the live Jewell partner site. It sits downstream of marketing-plan.html and applies brand-strategy.html and audience-teardown.html. It is deliberately narrow. Paid media is not the engine of Maxxim's near-term growth. The marketing plan is explicit that the model is proof-led and partner-led: the 3 July session, the cleared proof, the live Jewell partner, and the founder relationships do the persuading, and end-client demand generation is the partner's job on the partner's own channels. Paid sits behind organic and founder-led social as amplification, not the primary engine. This document specifies how to run the small, sharp paid layer that supports that motion, and where not to spend.

One frame governs every page. Near term, the paid priority is partner recruitment for the founding cohort, weighted to LinkedIn and aimed at trusted operators: the capacity-capped principals who would license the method or refer clients in. Search intent sits around agency enablement and AI-run marketing, which is where an operator evaluating this category actually looks. The secondary paid job is a small, always-ready end-client Growth Diagnostic intent layer on search that a partner or Jewell can switch on for the universal end-client audience. Everything else is held back.

The same discipline as every upstream document applies. All budgets, splits, caps, and spend figures here are illustrative, pending pilots, and require Clent sign-off before they are treated as fixed. This plan presents structure and approach, not a committed media budget. Real pricing anchors are stated as real: the Growth Diagnostic at $4,950 credited forward, the premium band of $25,000 to $250,000 with a recent run near $80,000, the specialist module near $1,500. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only cleared public Maxxim proof, so it is the only case study used in Maxxim-brand ads. OTR Earthmover is internal only, Hidrive is percentages only, and Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are never re-used as Maxxim's public proof in an ad. Ownership is Clent and Raef only. Maxxim's own category lead word is OPEN and is not invented or built into keyword targeting; Jewell's external lead word is growth. The 3 July session promise is OPEN and Clent's to finalise, so recruitment creative invites into the founding table rather than asserting a fixed promise. Australian English throughout, no em dashes, no exclamation marks.

What paid media is amplifying, in product terms

Paid does not sell a promise, it amplifies a showable engine. The 29 June product mechanics give the paid creative its substance and keep every claim inside what is real. This is the same substance the marketing plan and brand strategy lead with, applied to the paid layer.

  • Three named specialists, each a process. Max does the words in Claude Cowork, Sal does the pixels in the Design Studio, Pip runs the Social Studio. Naming them makes the engine concrete in a recruitment ad and gives the walkthrough video its content. They stay subordinate to Maxxim and are never the accountable human. The accountable human is always the partner.
  • The method is gated, and the gate is the proof. Discover, Design, Deploy, then Deepen, the compounding loop. Creative unlocks only as its briefs exist and a logo is locked. In a paid context, the gate is the visible answer to the trust objection, the thing a considered operator wants to see before they license.
  • Human in the loop is the point, not a limitation. AI carries the repeatable 80 percent at speed; the human owns the 20 percent clients pay for. This is the lead message angle for LinkedIn recruitment, tested against the proof-led Pottsville angle.
  • Finished, owned outcomes. Client sites are Astro on Cloudflare Pages, images from the Maxxim asset store, an embedded contact form now. The client owns the asset outright. In an end-client Diagnostic ad this is the credible differentiator against agency lock-in.
  • A live partner already runs the whole model. Jewell is in market on Maxxim as the engine behind it, which sharpens the recruitment pitch, but Jewell's named results are Jewell's, so paid creative references the partner model as pattern and does not lift Jewell's case studies into Maxxim-brand ads.
  • Built lean, on purpose. Only Max plus the engine is built in-house. The marketing consequence carries straight into paid: proof and relationships persuade, not a large media budget, which is exactly why this paid layer is small, sharp, and held back.

1. Paid Media Objectives

Paid media serves the marketing plan objectives, not its own. It has one primary job and one secondary job, and it is sized to a founding-cohort motion, not a demand-at-volume motion.

  • Primary business goal. Generate qualified operator interest for the founding cohort of 5 to 8 partners across July to September 2026: warm the recruitment pipeline, profile the 3 July session and the category, and drive session registrations and qualified operator enquiries. This supports marketing plan Objectives 1 and 2.
  • Secondary business goal. Capture end-client Growth Diagnostic intent through search, so that when a partner or Jewell wants to switch on demand for a real engagement, the $4,950 credited Diagnostic has a ready paid surface to catch high-intent search. This supports marketing plan Objective 3.
  • Explicit non-goal. Paid media does not run end-client demand generation at scale. That is the partner's job on the partner's own channels (marketing plan, Layer 2). Paid does not try to out-spend horizontal AI players, and it does not lead the brand.

Benchmarks and why they are ranges

Maxxim has no historical ad-account data, so no cost-per-acquisition or return-on-ad-spend benchmark can be stated as a baseline. The honest position is that the founding cohort is what converts these blanks into numbers. As illustrative external anchors only, B2B LinkedIn lead-generation cost per lead commonly sits in a wide band of roughly seventy-five to two hundred dollars or more for a considered, principal-level audience, and it should be read here as directional, not as a target Maxxim has committed to. The decisive economic number is not a media cost per acquisition at all. It is the value of a signed partner: a single licensed partner unlocks a delivery channel and recurring floor-plus-clip revenue, and again through the Deepen retainer once their clients are live (rates illustrative, pending pilots). Because one conversion is worth so much and the audience is so small and high-trust, a higher cost per qualified lead is acceptable here than in a volume consumer model.

Budget range and timeframe

Budget is deliberately modest and illustrative, consistent with the marketing plan's roughly 70 percent brand-and-community, 30 percent direct-response split. Paid sits inside the direct-response slice and inside the recruitment and enablement lines, not as a separate large budget. No total figure is committed here. The plan is built to scale to whatever modest monthly figure Clent signs off, with the structure below holding at any level. Timeframe is the founding-cohort window: a recruitment push now through the 3 July session, then sustained light spend across the July to September 2026 cohort window, with the search layer running always-on at a low floor.

Channels NOT recommended (and why)

Stating what not to run is as load-bearing as what to run, given a modest budget and a proof-led model.

  • Meta (Facebook and Instagram) for partner recruitment, not recommended now. The operator audience is reached better and more precisely on LinkedIn by seniority and firmographics. Meta's strength is consumer reach, which is not the Layer 1 job. A single small Meta awareness test to a matched operator list may be run from the test reserve, but Meta is not a core recruitment channel.
  • TikTok, not recommended. The audience is B2B principals making a considered licensing decision, not a short-form consumer audience. The budget does not support a third social platform, and the brand voice (no exclamation marks, claim-led, Sage) does not fit the platform. Explicitly do not use TikTok.
  • YouTube paid (in-stream and pre-roll), not recommended now. Video-first awareness at scale is premature for a founding-cohort motion and a thin proof base. The Max, Sal, and Pip walkthrough video should run as organic and as LinkedIn-native video to a tight audience, not as broad paid pre-roll.
  • Google Display prospecting and programmatic, not recommended. Broad display manufactures impressions, not qualified operator interest, and risks brand-safety exposure for a young brand that lives on credibility. Display is used only for warm retargeting, never cold prospecting.
  • Performance Max, not recommended now. It needs conversion volume and a clear value signal to optimise. Maxxim has neither yet. Revisit only once the search layer has accrued meaningful conversion data and the Diagnostic value is confirmed.
  • Paid end-client demand generation at volume, not recommended by design. Layer 2 demand is the partner's job on the partner's own channels. Maxxim-run paid stops at intent capture and partner support. This also holds the OPEN end-client visibility and ownership questions where the marketing plan leaves them, rather than pre-empting them with a spend commitment.

4. Campaign Structure

A lean account structure a media buyer can stand up directly. Two ad accounts, a single naming convention, and a clean prospecting-versus-retargeting split inside each platform.

  • Account structure. One LinkedIn Campaign Manager account and one Google Ads account, both under Maxxim, with conversion tracking unified through the Maxxim tag setup (Section 9). No separate partner sub-accounts at this stage; partner-run local search campaigns, if any, sit in the partner's own account and are out of scope for this Maxxim-run plan.
  • Naming convention. Maxxim_[Platform]_[Objective]_[Audience]_[YYYY-MM]. For example, Maxxim_LinkedIn_Recruit_OperatorCold_2026-07, Maxxim_LinkedIn_Recruit_Retarget_2026-07, Maxxim_Google_Diagnostic_Intent_2026-07, Maxxim_Google_BrandDefence_2026-07. The convention feeds clean reporting and the lead-source attribution the revenue model depends on.
  • Campaign to ad set or ad group segmentation. On LinkedIn, separate campaigns for cold prospecting (firmographic plus interest), matched-list and lookalike, and retargeting, so budgets and bids are controlled per audience temperature. On Google, separate ad groups by keyword theme (diagnostic intent, local service, brand defence) so message match is tight and quality score holds.
  • Audience segmentation within campaigns. Prospecting and retargeting never share a campaign. Tiers run warm to cold: matched named-pipeline list and site converters first (highest priority), then lookalike and interest prospecting, then broad firmographic. Retargeting is its own line with its own creative.
  • Geographic and scheduling settings. Australia first, aligned to the founder network; widen only as the cohort proves out. Schedule LinkedIn delivery to business hours and weekdays, where principals engage, during the learning phase, then open if data supports it. The search layer runs continuously at a low floor so high-intent Diagnostic queries are never missed.

Creative testing plan

Test in priority order, one variable at a time so the read is clean. First, message angle on LinkedIn cold: the engine-for-your-judgement (80/20) claim against the proof-led Pottsville angle, because the winning angle shapes everything downstream. Second, format: single image against the Max, Sal, and Pip walkthrough video, to learn whether demonstration outperforms a static claim for this audience. Third, CTA and capture path: native Lead Gen Form against a click to the session landing page, to learn where qualified operators convert more cheaply. On Google, test headline theme (Diagnostic outcome versus named-human accountability versus speed) once volume allows. Audience tests (matched list versus lookalike versus interest) run in parallel at the campaign level, not blended into the creative test. Launch every ad group with at least two to three variants; never run a single creative.

9. Tracking and Attribution

Measurement is specified so a media buyer can set up tracking from this document alone, and so paid reconciles cleanly with the marketing plan's two-layer attribution. The single most important discipline is unchanged from the marketing plan: the lead-source rule tags every engagement partner-sourced, Maxxim-sourced, or referred before delivery begins, because that tag drives the clip and the finder's fee. Paid attribution feeds that rule; it does not replace it.

  • Conversion tracking setup. Install the Google tag (gtag or GTM) and the LinkedIn Insight Tag on maxxim.ai, plus the Google Ads conversion tag. Configure GA4 as the cross-platform source of truth, with platform-native reporting used for in-platform optimisation. All tags load through a consent-gated tag manager.
  • Conversion events. Define and track: 3 July session registration (primary Layer 1 event), qualified operator enquiry (Layer 1), Diagnostic enquiry submit via the embedded contact form (Layer 2), and Max, Sal, and Pip walkthrough video 50 percent view (a soft, retargeting-only signal). Assign a nominal lead value to qualified operator enquiry to let bidding optimise toward quality, recognising the true value is a signed partner, not a media conversion.
  • UTM conventions. A fixed structure on every paid link: utm_source (linkedin, google), utm_medium (paid-social, cpc), utm_campaign matching the campaign name (for example maxxim_linkedin_recruit_operatorcold_2026-07), utm_content (creative variant), utm_term (keyword, search only). Consistent UTMs are what let GA4 reconcile the platforms and what feed the lead-source tag.
  • Attribution model. For Layer 1 recruitment, use first-touch-plus-confirmed-source, matching the marketing plan: record where a partner first heard of Maxxim and confirm the source at sign, because the decision is near-solo and relationship-led, not a multi-touch web journey. For Layer 2 Diagnostic intent, use data-driven attribution in GA4 where volume permits, falling back to last-click while volume is thin. Neither paid model overrides the commercial lead-source rule that drives the clip.
  • Cross-platform reconciliation. GA4 is the source of truth for cross-channel reporting; LinkedIn and Google native reporting are used only for in-platform bid and creative decisions. Expect platform-reported conversions to over-count against GA4; reconcile to GA4 for any spend or return conversation.
  • Privacy and consent (fundamental, not a footnote). A consent management banner and Google consent mode v2 must be live before paid tracking is trusted, consistent with the privacy-respecting posture of the Astro-on-Cloudflare site. iOS and browser tracking restrictions mean retargeting pools will be smaller and view-through data weaker than platform dashboards imply; plan for server-side tagging where feasible to improve signal. For any end-client data captured, honour public-sector and not-for-profit privacy obligations where a partner activates those audiences. No tracking that consent does not cover is recommended; if it cannot be measured cleanly and compliantly, it is not run.
