Brand Strategy

How to read this document

This is the strategic brand platform for Maxxim, produced in the Design phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions in discover-addendum.html, and the 1 July refresh that folded in the 29 June product mechanics and the live Jewell partner site. It is the reference everything downstream is built on: website, copy, campaigns, the partner hub, the 3 July community session. It defines positioning, personality, voice, messaging, and architecture. It does not design the logo or set the palette; those are locked and stated here only as the strategic frame the brand must fit. It draws on discover-summary.html, audience-teardown.html, competitor-analysis.html, and offer-worksheet.html.

What is locked is stated plainly. What is open stays open. Per v2.1 and the Gate 1 decisions, this document does not invent Maxxim's own category lead word, partner economics numbers, the front-door product, the fractional CMO model, Maxxim's end-client visibility, the 3 July community promise wording, or Christy Kilmartin's role split. Where a choice is Clent's to make, options are offered and framed as his call. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only cleared public Maxxim proof, OTR Earthmover is internal only, Hidrive is percentages only, and Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner.

The seam this brand is built on

Since the 27 June version, one thing has gone from a model on paper to a working example in market. The human-20 percent and machine-80 percent split, the core of the whole business, is now concretely embodied by the live Jewell partner. Jewell enters and owns the client. The Discover phase is always human. Design and Deploy hand off to the Maxxim engine at scale once the judgement work is set, and every stage is human-checked. Jewell is the name on the door. Maxxim is the engine behind it. That is the single clearest articulation of the brand's job, and it governs every section below.

The strategic bet this seam encodes is explicit in the product guide: good, fast, cheap stops being a trade-off when the human stays exactly where quality is decided. AI carries the repeatable volume; the human holds trust, taste, and judgement; AI proposes and the human decides and signs off. The brand's whole job is to make that split obvious, credible, and ownable, and to make the human the hero rather than the machine.

The operating stack this brand sits on

Before the strategy, the structure. The brand has to make five layers legible at once, because the customer is the partner and the value is the end client served through them. Layer 1 owns the method, Layer 5 receives the outcome. The three named specialists, Max, Sal, and Pip, are how the method becomes visible product, and they live inside Layer 3.

One structural note the brand must carry: the partner is the front door now, with the end-user self-serve portal as the north star, opened later once partner-led delivery proves the method and produces the pilot economics. The brand has to work for the partner today and stretch to a product-led, self-serve future without re-branding. Which layer Maxxim leads with in market is an OPEN tension, not settled here.

1. Brand Foundation

Purpose

To make agency-grade growth repeatable, so that a proven method and a trusted human are no longer capped by one person's hours.

Vision

A community of trusted partners, each able to commercialise any organisation, runs Jewell's method at scale, and the human judgement clients pay for compounds across the network instead of burning out inside one operator.

Mission

We give trusted operators the engine to deliver Jewell's gated 3D Process at scale, pairing AI that runs the repeatable 80 percent at speed with an accountable human who owns the 20 percent that clients actually pay for.

Values

Five values, each written as a behaviour rather than a platitude, drawn from the discovery direction and the v2.1 guardrails. Wording is for Clent to confirm; the direction is settled.

  • A human stands behind it. Every output carries a named person's accountability. If no one will put their name on it, it does not ship. This is the answer to the central trust gap, not a slogan.
  • Finished, not busy. We sell launched outcomes and owned assets, not hours, dashboards, or activity. The phone ringing beats the deck looking good.
  • The client owns it. The end client owns their assets outright, hosted on their own domain and unlockable to hand-editable HTML. We do not hold the keys. Ownership is the opposite of the agency lock-in the market resents.
  • Keep the gate. The gated process is the quality bar, and the product enforces it: creative unlocks only as its briefs exist and a logo is locked. No phase proceeds until the prior one is signed off. The gates are what let a partner trust the machine.
  • Stronger together. Trust, taste, and judgement held by a community beat the same held by a lone operator. We build the table, online and offline, because the community is the moat.

2. Brand Positioning Statement

The positioning is written for the primary customer, the trusted partner, because v2.1 and the Gate 1 decisions name the partner as the front door now. The end-client benefit is the proof the partner buys into, so it sits inside the reason to believe rather than competing for the lead. The live Jewell partner is the proof that a real operator can run the whole model, which sharpens every claim below.

For trusted operators who can win the work and own the relationship but cannot deliver agency-grade growth repeatably, Maxxim is the commercialisation engine that runs Jewell's proven, gated 3D Process at AI speed with an accountable human at every gate. Unlike agency-in-a-box tooling that hands over a platform with no method, or pure-AI stacks that hand over speed with no one to stand behind it, Maxxim pairs a method that has already worked for years with three named specialists, Max, Sal, and Pip, and a community of partners who share the trust, taste, and judgement that clients actually pay for. The result a partner can promise, and put their name on: a complete strategy and launched assets the client owns outright, delivered in days not months, for any organisation they choose to commercialise. The engine behind the partner, not the name on the door.

Target Audience

Primary: the Capacity-Capped Operator, the Client Partner at the licence door (Segment A1 in the Audience Teardown). A skilled marketing or business operator with a network, a reputation, and judgement, but no repeatable machine, signing at principal level. Alex and 3P, with around 36 named opportunities, and Arran Aitken with a tradie database, are the live shape. Jewell itself, a 35-year operator now running the full model in market, is the proof that this operator can become a scaled partner rather than a stuck one. Secondary partner audiences are the Networked Referrer (A2) and the Scoped Specialist (A3). The end client served through the partner is now explicitly any organisation type: personal brand, solo operator, SME, corporate, not for profit, and all levels of government.

Category / Frame of Reference

The category Maxxim competes in is a commercialisation engine and growth platform delivered through trusted partners. It is the trustworthy middle between slow, expensive, opaque agencies and risky, effort-heavy pure-AI tools. Maxxim's own one-word category lead remains OPEN and is not invented here (see Section 6 for framed options that are Clent's call). Jewell's external lead word, by contrast, is locked: growth. Maxxim's category word must not simply borrow it.

Point of Difference

The single most ownable thing: Maxxim is the only model in the competitive set that is fast and accountable and method-backed at the same time, delivering finished, owned outcomes. Agencies have the human without the speed. AI tools have the speed without the human. Enablement platforms have reach without a method. No category combines all three. This is the empty quadrant from the Competitor Analysis white-space read, and it is the spine of the brand. Two things make it hard to copy: the method is enforced by the product, not just promised, and the human 20 percent is now proven in market by a live partner.

Reason to Believe

  • A method proven for years, then enforced by the product. The 3D Process has run with real clients for years through Jewell, and the platform now enforces it mechanically, so the gates are a working guarantee rather than a claim.
  • A live partner running the whole model. Jewell is in market on Maxxim as the engine behind it, the first working proof that the partner model holds. Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are not re-used as Maxxim's public proof.
  • Pottsville Acupuncture, launched and owned. A full 3D run for a local allied-health business, with a launched website the client owns outright. The only cleared public Maxxim proof, and the lead case study.
  • A premium run transacted at around $80,000 inside the $25,000 to $250,000 band, evidence the top of the ladder is real, not theoretical.
  • Three named specialists doing real production. Max does the words in Cowork, Sal does the pixels in the Design Studio, Pip runs the Social Studio. The engine is showable, not a black box.
  • The human moat. Trust, taste, and judgement held by a partner community, compounding across runs, is an advantage a lone operator or a horizontal AI suite cannot assemble quickly.

Brand Promise

License a proven method and run agency-grade growth at scale under your own name, keep the margin, and stand behind every result, for any organisation you choose to commercialise.

The "could a competitor say this" test. An agency cannot claim AI speed. An AI tool cannot claim an accountable human at every gate. An enablement platform cannot claim a proven method with cleared proof, enforced by the product. Only Maxxim can claim all three at once, plus a live partner already running it and a partner community as the moat. The positioning passes.

3. Brand Personality

Archetype Selection

A blend of two, weighted, not a single archetype in a straitjacket.

  • Primary: the Sage. Maxxim's authority comes from a method proven over years and a refusal to dress plumbing up as the product. The Sage values truth, mastery, and judgement over spectacle. This fits the audience precisely: operators judge the engine, not the marketing, and the central objection is closed by evidence, not enthusiasm. The Sage is also the right posture for the wider aperture set at Gate 1, because government and not-for-profit buyers reward probity and proof over flash. The trust-taste-judgement thesis is a Sage argument at its core.
  • Supporting: the Creator, expressed as the builder of an engine. Maxxim makes the machine that lets others make finished things. This is the productising instinct, the gated system, the three named specialists, the compounding loop. It keeps the Sage from feeling academic and ties the brand to tangible, launched outcomes.

What the brand deliberately is not: the Magician promising AI transformation, or the Hero promising to slay the agency. Both over-claim, and over-claim is exactly what the trust gap punishes. The human moat, the case that a community is stronger than a lone operator, also pulls the brand gently toward an Everyman warmth in how the community is spoken about, without diluting the Sage authority at the core.

Personality Traits

  • Confident but not boastful. States what is proven, flags what is open, never inflates.
  • Plain-spoken but precise. Operator to operator, short sentences, exact vocabulary, no filler.
  • Accountable, visibly. Names the human, shows the gate, owns the standard. The human is the hero, not the machine.
  • Grounded, not hyped. Substance over spectacle. Plumbing is not the product.
  • Generous within the community. Warm and reciprocal with partners, because the table is the moat, while staying disciplined and commercial.

Tone-of-Voice Descriptors

Direct. Grounded. Credible. Calm.

4. Brand Voice Guide

Four dimensions, each with a do and a don't a copywriter can apply immediately. Every example obeys the locked external style: no em dashes, no exclamation marks, Australian English.

DimensionMore like thisLess like this
ConfidenceAssured and evidence-backed. Claims are sized to the proof.Boastful or hyperbolic. Claims outrun the evidence.
FormalityPlain operator-to-operator. Short sentences, exact words.Stiff corporate jargon, or loose and chatty filler.
AccountabilityNames the human and shows the gate. The human is the hero.Faceless and automated. The machine is the hero.
HonestyStates what is locked, flags what is open, no false certainty.Papering over open questions with invented precision.

Do and don't examples

  • Confidence. Do: "The method has run with real clients for years. Pottsville is launched, and the client owns it. Jewell now runs the whole model in market." Don't: "Maxxim is the revolutionary AI platform transforming marketing forever."
  • Formality. Do: "AI runs the repeatable 80 percent at speed. You own the 20 percent clients pay for." Don't: "Our synergistic end-to-end solution leverages cutting-edge artificial intelligence to optimise outcomes."
  • Accountability. Do: "A named human signs off at every gate, so the work is something you can stand behind. The engine behind the partner, not the name on the door." Don't: "Our AI handles everything automatically, no human needed."
  • Honesty. Do: "Partner economics are set in structure. The exact floor and clip are being proven through the founding cohort." Don't: "Partners keep 70 percent, guaranteed." (Inventing an open number breaks the rules and the trust.)

Naming the specialists in copy

Max, Sal, and Pip are the branded expression of the method: Max does the words, Sal does the pixels, Pip runs social. Name them when it makes the engine concrete for a partner, and keep them subordinate to Maxxim. They are how the method is shown working; they are never presented as separate products or as the accountable human. The accountable human is always the partner.

Channel-specific tone adaptations

  • Website and partner hub: the fullest expression of the Sage. Lead with the position, then the proof, then the recommendation. Confident and grounded. This is the proof surface, so let cleared evidence and the live partner do the persuading.
  • The 3 July community session and partner conversations: warmer, more Everyman, more reciprocal. This is the table, not a pitch. Run as discovery and alignment, invite rather than sell. The exact community promise wording is OPEN and Clent's to finalise.
  • End-client-facing copy (delivered through partners): simpler and more outcome-led than partner copy. The tradie or local SME wants the phone to ring. Lead with done for you, owned by you, live in days. Government and not-for-profit framings lean harder on probity, accountability, and ownership.
  • Proposals and gate sign-offs: most formal and most precise. Separate locked from open explicitly. This is where accountability and honesty carry the most weight.
  • Social and short form: the Sage compressed. One sharp, true claim per post. No exclamation marks means emphasis comes from the claim itself, not punctuation.

5. Visual Identity Direction

The visual identity is LOCKED: Signal Blue #2D5BFF, Poppins, design Edition 03, Blue Frog removed. This section gives the strategic rationale the locked identity already satisfies, so downstream design briefs apply it with intent rather than re-opening it. A reversed wordmark now exists as an inverse variant within this locked identity; it is a variant, not a new identity.

Colour Psychology Rationale

Signal Blue #2D5BFF occupies the right emotional territory for the brand: blue is the colour of trust, reliability, and competence, which is exactly the ground the trust gap is fought on, and a bright, saturated, electric blue adds the speed and modernity that signal AI capability. Together they encode the core promise, trust plus speed, in a single colour. The "Signal" name reinforces accountability and clarity, a clear signal rather than noise. Keep the palette disciplined and high-contrast, in keeping with a Sage brand that values legibility over decoration.

Typography Direction

Poppins is a geometric sans serif: modern, clean, even, and approachable without being playful or decorative. It carries the operator-to-operator plainness of the voice and the builder precision of the Creator support archetype. Use weight and hierarchy, not ornament, to create emphasis, which suits a brand whose external style strips out exclamation marks and leans on the strength of the claim. Generous spacing and a clear typographic scale keep it credible across the partner hub, proposals, and end-client sites.

Imagery Style Guide

Real over illustrated, candid over staged, outcome over abstract. The brand sells finished, owned outcomes, so imagery should show real launched work and real people accountable for it, the Pottsville site, the named human, the partner at the table, rather than stock AI abstractions of glowing brains and circuit boards. Abstract AI imagery undercuts the human-at-the-wheel position. Where the engine is shown, show the structured output and the gate, and the specialists Max, Sal, and Pip as a legible system, not a black box. For the universal-organisation aperture, imagery should flex across a tradie, a not-for-profit, and a government context without feeling like a single vertical.

Logo Usage Principles

Digital-first, so the mark must hold up small, in a browser tab, an app icon, a partner-hub avatar, and in single-colour Signal Blue on white and white on Signal Blue. With Blue Frog removed, the system is cleaner and more typographic, which fits the Sage restraint. A reversed wordmark variant now exists for dark grounds and Signal Blue fields: the white "maxxim" logotype on transparent, for use where the standard mark would lose contrast. It carries its own clear-space and contrast notes in the Logo Brief and Brand Guidelines. It stays inside the locked identity, Signal Blue, Poppins, Edition 03, and is a variant, not a re-opening of the identity. Protect clear space, never stretch or recolour outside the locked palette, and keep the wordmark legible against busy end-client contexts. The mark should read as a platform and a standard, not a mascot.

7. Brand Architecture

Architecture Model

Recommendation: a branded house with three endorsed specialists. Maxxim is the master brand and the platform. Max, Sal, and Pip are named specialists inside it, each a process, endorsed by and subordinate to Maxxim, never separate brands. Jewell is a distinct, related brand that sits above as the method owner and endorses Maxxim ("the engine for Jewell's 3D Process"), in the way an ingredient or a standards body endorses a product, and it is now also a live partner running on the platform. This is a deliberate choice not to run a house of brands: a house of brands would fracture a young brand's scarce proof and trust across multiple names, which the Competitor Analysis flags as the single biggest vulnerability. One strong name carries all the proof.
