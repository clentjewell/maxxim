Copy Deck

How to read this document

This is the messaging framework for Maxxim, produced in the Design phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions in discover-summary.html, and the locked brand-strategy.html. It was refreshed on 1 July to fold in two new inputs: the 29 June product guide, which gives the real mechanics of how the platform works day to day, and the live Jewell partner site, which is the first in-market proof of the whole model. This is not finished ad copy. It is the layer the website, the partner hub, the social channels, and the 3 July showcase materials are written from. Every channel specialist and creative should be able to write on brand without re-deriving the strategy, by lifting the hierarchy, the audience messages, the headline patterns, and the call-to-action language set out here. It draws on offer-worksheet.html, audience-teardown.html, and customer-profile.html.

It carries two audiences at once, because Maxxim sells to a partner who serves an end client. The partner is the front door now: the operator who licenses or refers, the human moat, the community. The end client is universal: personal brand, solo operator, SME, corporate, not for profit, and all levels of government, reached through the partner. The single objection under both is the trust gap. The sharpest way to state the whole brand in one line, now that a real operator embodies it, is this: the engine behind the partner, not the name on the door. The lead word for end-client growth copy is locked: growth.

What is open stays open. Per v2.1 and Gate 1, this deck does not invent Maxxim's one-word category lead, partner economics percentages, the front-door product claim, the fractional CMO model for Maxxim, Maxxim's end-client visibility rule, the showcase promise wording, or Christy Kilmartin's role split. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only launched public proof and leads all copy; OTR Earthmover is internal only; Hidrive is percentages only; and Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are never re-used as Maxxim's own public proof. House style is locked and hard: no em dashes, no exclamation marks, Australian English, operator to operator, confident and plain, never hype. The business is Jewell, never Joule.

The seam every message is written across

Since the 27 June version, the core of the business went from a model on paper to a working example in market. The human-20-percent and machine-80-percent split is now concretely embodied by the live Jewell partner. Jewell enters and owns the client, the Discover phase is always human, then Design and Deploy hand off to the Maxxim engine at scale, human-checked at every stage. Jewell is the name on the door. Maxxim is the engine behind it. That sentence governs the tone of every block below: the human is always the hero, the machine is always the volume, and the copy never lets the machine take the credit the human is accountable for.

The engine is now nameable and showable. Three named specialists, each one a process, make the method concrete: Max does the words in Claude Cowork (strategy, plans, copy, briefs, summaries); Sal does the pixels in the Design Studio (logos, brand imagery, collateral, video); Pip runs the Social Studio (drafts and, on approval, publishes posts). The work is gated and mechanical: creative unlocks only as the briefs it depends on exist and a logo is locked, and the Social Studio unlocks only once the Social Strategy exists. Clients receive a complete strategy and launched assets they own outright, on their own domain, in days not months. When copy needs to prove the engine is real rather than a claim, it reaches for these facts.

1. Messaging Hierarchy

One primary message at the top, three supporting messages beneath it, proof points at the base. Everything in market should ladder up to this without a rewrite per channel. The hierarchy is the partner-side argument first, because the partner is the buyer; the end-client benefit sits inside the proof the partner buys into. It is tight enough to fit on a single slide.

Primary message

Maxxim is the proven method, run by a human, at AI speed: the engine that lets a trusted operator deliver agency-grade growth repeatably, and put their name on every result. The engine behind the partner, not the name on the door.

Supporting messages and their proof

  • Pillar 1, the engine for your judgement (the 80/20). Max, Sal, and Pip run the repeatable 80 percent at speed, you own the 20 percent clients actually pay for. Proof: the 3D Process has run with real clients for years through Jewell; Max does the words, Sal does the pixels, Pip runs social; the end client gets finished, owned work in days not months.
  • Pillar 2, the human at every gate (the trust gap). A named human signs off at every phase, so the work is something you can stand behind. Proof: Pottsville Acupuncture launched and owned by the client; the gates are enforced by the product, not just promised, because creative unlocks only as its briefs exist and a logo is locked; a recent premium run transacted at around $80,000 inside the $25,000 to $250,000 band.
  • Pillar 3, the community is the moat (stronger together). Trust, taste, and judgement held by a community beat the same held by a lone operator. Proof: a founding cohort of 5 to 8 partners proving the model; Jewell already live in market on Maxxim as the engine behind it, the first working proof the partner model holds; a shared table, shared deal flow, and a compounding Deepen loop that improves the method for everyone.

2. Value Proposition Statement

For trusted operators who can win the work but cannot deliver agency-grade growth repeatably, Maxxim is the commercialisation engine that runs a proven, gated method at AI speed with an accountable human at every gate, so you can scale under your own name, keep your margin, and stand behind every result, for any organisation you choose to commercialise.

This framing leads with the partner because Gate 1 names the partner as the front door now, and it answers the three questions a value proposition must answer: what Maxxim does (runs a proven method fast, with a human accountable), for whom (trusted operators who are capacity-capped), and why it matters (scale, margin, and a result you can put your name on). It was chosen over an end-client-led framing, which would speak past the actual buyer, and over a pure-AI-speed framing, which would walk straight into the trust gap the brand exists to close. The live Jewell partner is now the proof this is not theory: a 35-year operator runs the whole model in market, which lets the proposition end on a fact rather than a promise.

Naming the specialists in copy

Max, Sal, and Pip are the branded expression of the method: Max does the words, Sal does the pixels, Pip runs social. Name them when it makes the engine concrete for a partner or a scrutinising buyer, and keep them subordinate to Maxxim. They are how the method is shown working. They are never presented as separate products, and never as the accountable human. The accountable human is always the partner. When copy would tempt a reader to think the machine is in charge, name the partner instead of the specialist.

Channel-specific tone adaptations

  • Website and partner hub: the fullest expression of the Sage. Lead with the position, then the proof, then the recommendation. Confident and grounded. This is the proof surface, so let cleared evidence and the live Jewell partner do the persuading. Add Pottsville as the lead case. Do not display OTR, Hidrive, BTC, or Jewell Tyres, and do not re-use Jewell's named client results as Maxxim's own public proof.
  • Email (partner nurture and end-client): short and substantive. One claim per message, a single clear next step. Partner email is operator-to-operator and reciprocal; end-client email is outcome-led and reassuring. Transactional email stays plain and exact, especially around the Growth Diagnostic credit.
  • Social and short form: the Sage compressed. One sharp, true claim per post. No exclamation marks means emphasis comes from the claim itself, not punctuation. Lift lines straight from Sections 1 and 4. Never imply economics that are still open.
  • Sales collateral, proposals, and gate sign-offs: the most formal and most precise register. Separate locked from open explicitly. This is where accountability and honesty carry the most weight. For public-sector buyers, lean into documented method and compliance language.
  • The 3 July showcase and partner conversations: warmer, more reciprocal. This is the table, not a pitch. Run as discovery and alignment, invite rather than sell. The exact showcase promise wording is open and Clent's to finalise, so copy stays directional, not committed.

8. Call-to-Action Library

Organised by funnel stage, split across the partner door and the end-client door, because the two motions convert differently. Each CTA names where to use it and why the language works at that stage. The two primary CTAs are set: the partner converts on the founding cohort, the end client converts on the Growth Diagnostic.

Awareness, first touch

  • See how it works. Use on social, homepage nav, and first-touch ads. Low commitment, invites the curious to understand the model before judging it.
  • Meet the engine for your judgement. Use on partner-facing first-touch. Names the operator's job to be done, not a tool, so it earns the click without over-claiming.
  • See the method, run by a human. Use on end-client first-touch. Leads with the trust position, which is the first thing the wary buyer needs.

Consideration, evaluation stage

  • Read the Pottsville story. Use on the Work page and in nurture. Leads with the only cleared public proof, which is exactly what a scrutinising buyer wants at this stage.
  • Meet Max, Sal, and Pip. Use on How It Works and partner consideration pages. The partner judges the engine by seeing the named specialists at work, so the CTA offers a look inside, not a claim.
  • See what the gates cover. Use on How It Works. Surfaces the accountability mechanism that closes the trust gap, for buyers comparing options.

Decision, conversion points

  • Join the founding partner cohort. Primary partner CTA. Use on For Partners and at the close of the showcase materials. Frames the next step as joining a founding table, with genuine cohort scarcity, not buying a tool.
  • Book your Growth Diagnostic. Primary end-client CTA. Use on end-client pages and partner-led proposals. Carries the locked lead word, growth, and points to the only fully settled price, the credited $4,950 first step, which makes the first yes low risk.
  • Start a partner conversation. Use where a softer entry suits the referrer or specialist. An invitation to a discovery and alignment chat, not a commitment, which fits the lighter doors.
