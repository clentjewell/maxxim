Website Strategy

How to read this document

This is the strategic blueprint for the refresh of maxxim.ai, produced in the Design phase against Master Briefing v2.1, the Gate 1 decisions in discover-addendum.html, and the 1 July refresh that folded in the 29 June product mechanics and the live Jewell partner site. It defines what the site must achieve, how it is structured, who travels through it and how, what each priority page must do, and how visitors convert. It is not a wireframe or a visual design, and it is not finished copy. It is the structure the Deploy-phase build and copy teams work from. It sits alongside brand-strategy.html, copy-deck.html, customer-profile.html, and offer-worksheet.html, and hands forward to website-brief.html in Deploy.

The site already exists, and since the 27 June version we now know exactly what it is built on. maxxim.ai is a live Astro site hosted on Cloudflare Pages, the same delivery stack every Maxxim client site ships on. The live pages are Home, How it works, For Partners, About, Work, Start, Contact, Privacy, and Terms. This strategy does not start from a blank canvas. It does two things at once: it sets the strategic direction for the refreshed site, and it carries the four required fixes from the live-site audit (Master Briefing Section 16) into the information architecture and the content plan so they are corrected by design rather than as an afterthought.

Two framing decisions from Gate 1 govern everything here. First, the partner is the primary front door now, so the site needs a strong, unambiguous For Partners path, with Jewell as the first and live exemplar partner, while the site still has to serve the universal end-client audience the partner commercialises. Second, the self-serve portal is the north star, a roadmap item, not a current page. It is named on the site as a direction of travel, never built or linked as if it exists.

Three guardrails are absolute throughout. The business is Jewell, never "Joule", in any live-copy recommendation. Ownership is Clent and Raef only, and Paul Clancy is a Client Partner, never a co-founder. And all partner economics stay unquoted and illustrative. Proof respects clearance: Pottsville Acupuncture is the only public Maxxim case study. The live Jewell partner site is a valuable reference and pattern for how the seam is articulated, but Jewell's own named results stay under Jewell's banner and are never re-used as Maxxim's public proof. BTC, OTR Earthmover, Hidrive, and Jewell Tyres are not to be published as Maxxim proof.

The seam the site has to make legible

The whole business is one split, and the site's first job is to make it obvious in seconds. AI runs the repeatable 80 percent at machine speed. A human partner owns the 20 percent clients actually pay for: trust, taste, judgement, and the relationship. As of the 1 July refresh this is no longer a model on paper. The live Jewell partner runs the entire model in market, which gives the site a concrete pattern to point at and a fact to end on rather than a promise. The engine is now nameable too: Max does the words in Claude Cowork, Sal does the pixels in the Design Studio, Pip runs the Social Studio. These specialists let the site show the engine instead of asserting it, and they always stay subordinate to the accountable human, who is the partner.

1. Website Objectives

Five objectives, each tied to a business goal from the operating model and the offer, each with a way to know whether it is met. The site is a conversion system with two front doors at very different commitment levels: a high-consideration partner enquiry, and a low-risk end-client entry product.

ObjectiveBusiness goal linkSuccess metric
1. Make the partner the primary front door. Convert trusted operators into a partner enquiry, leading toward the founding cohort and the two-door model. Partner-led delivery is the near-term go-to-market and proves the method and pilot economics first (Gate 1 Decision 2; founding cohort of 5 to 8, July to September 2026). Partner enquiries submitted per month; share of enquiries that reach a partner discovery conversation; founding-cohort places filled.
2. Drive the end-client entry product. Give partners and direct visitors a clean way to book the $4,950 Growth Diagnostic as the universal first step. The Growth Diagnostic is the recommended universal front-door step and the only fully settled price, generating the repeatable first sale that produces pilot data (Gate 1 Decision 3; Offer Worksheet Section 7). Growth Diagnostic enquiries and bookings; assisted conversions where a partner uses the page to close a client.
3. Close the trust gap with cleared proof and a showable engine. Make the case that AI can hit a standard a partner will put their name on, using Pottsville, the gated method, and the named specialists. The single objection under every persona on both layers is the trust gap; the highest-value brand action is closing the cleared-proof gap (Brand Strategy Section 8; Customer Profile). Visits to and engagement with the Work and How it works pages; scroll depth on the proof block and the Max/Sal/Pip block; proof pages in the path of converting sessions.
4. Make the community a reason to join. Present the partner community as the human moat, not a feature, so partners stay for the table as much as the tool. The community is the durable advantage in an age where AI commoditises the first 80 percent (Discover Summary, the human moat; Brand Strategy Pillar 3). Engagement with the community section of For Partners; community framing cited in partner enquiry messages; showcase sign-ups.
5. Be correct and credible. Carry the four Section 16 fixes so the live site stops contradicting the brand truth (Jewell not Joule, Clent and Raef only, Pottsville as proof, the two-door model). A young brand with thin public proof cannot afford factual or naming errors; correctness is a precondition for trust (Master Briefing Section 16; Brand Strategy Section 8). Zero instances of "Joule" or "co-founder Paul Clancy" post-refresh; Pottsville live on Work; two-door model live on For Partners.

2. Sitemap / Information Architecture

The refresh keeps the existing page set, which is sound, and re-roles it around the partner-first front door. The mental model the navigation must match is the primary visitor's: a trusted operator deciding whether to put their name on this engine. So the primary navigation leads with the proof of how it works and what it has produced, then the partner door, then who stands behind it. The end-client entry product lives on a dedicated, linkable page (Start) that a partner can send a client straight to.

Primary navigation (justified)

  • How it works. The primary partner judges the engine, not the marketing. This page shows the gated 3D method, Discover through Deepen, the 80/20 split, the three named specialists at work in the Studios, and a human signing off at every gate. It earns its top-nav place because it is where the trust gap starts to close.
  • Work. Proof is the highest-value asset for a lightly proven brand. Pottsville is the cleared public case, a launched site the client owns outright on the same stack the visitor is looking at. Kept in the primary nav because cleared proof carries more weight than any claim.
  • For Partners. The partner is the front door, so the partner door is prominent in the primary nav. This is where the two-door model and the community live, and where the strongest, most deliberate path on the site runs.
  • About. Who owns and stands behind the method. Carries the ownership correction and the Paul Clancy reframe. A Sage brand needs a credible, accurate About.
  • Start. The end-client entry page for the $4,950 Growth Diagnostic. In the nav so a partner can point a client straight to it, and so a direct end-client visitor has a clear front-door product.
  • Contact. The partner enquiry destination and the general enquiry catch-all. Holds the primary conversion form, embedded from apps.maxxim.ai.

Secondary and utility pages

  • No deep child pages are recommended for phase 1, with one optional exception: the Pottsville case may live at its own URL under Work if a dedicated case page is wanted (see Section 8). The site is otherwise ruthlessly scoped: six primary pages, two of which are conversion pages, plus legals. A two-owner operation backed by a specialist bench does not need a forty-page site, and a flat structure suits the short, decisive partner journey.
  • Utility: Privacy and Terms in the footer, alongside a footer Contact link. These already exist and stay.

Rationale and what is deferred

The hierarchy follows the primary visitor's decision sequence, not the org chart: understand the method, see the proof, choose a door, check who is behind it, then act. Two doors are deliberately separated rather than merged, because a partner enquiry and an end-client booking are different commitments at different price points and must not compete on the same button. The self-serve portal is deferred to a roadmap mention only; it is named as the north star on Home and How it works and is never linked or built. A partner hub or dashboard (the logged-in community surface, the weekly table, the deal-share board, the directory) is referenced on For Partners as what a partner gets, but the hub itself is a Deploy or post-Deploy build behind authentication, extending the existing admin.maxxim.ai dashboard, not a public page in this IA. The read-only client portal and the assistant Ari are a partner-choice surface for end clients, not a public marketing page, and are not in this nav. A future resources or insights section is a phase-2 candidate, deferred until there is cleared content and capacity to maintain it.

3. User Flows

Two primary flows, one per front door, mapped to the personas in the Customer Profile. The partner-prospect flow is the priority because the partner is the front door now. The end-client flow is partner-mediated in most cases, but the page must also serve a direct end-client visitor and, above all, give a partner a clean link to close a client.

Flow A: Partner prospect (primary)

  • Persona: Operator Owen, the Capacity-Capped Client Partner (Segment A1, primary). The Networked Referrer (Connector Carol) follows a lighter version of the same flow, exiting at the refer door on For Partners.
  • Entry point: founder-network referral, the 3 July showcase invitation, operator-to-operator word of mouth, seeing the live Jewell partner site as the worked example, and direct or search arrival at maxxim.ai. Owen trusts demonstration over decks, so he arrives ready to scrutinise.
  • Key pages: Home, How it works, Work, For Partners, Contact. About is a side-trip to verify who is behind it.
  • Conversion point: a partner enquiry submitted on Contact (or via the primary CTA carried through the nav), leading to a partner discovery conversation.
  • Critical path: How it works to Work to For Partners to Contact. This sequence moves Owen from understanding the engine, to scrutinising the proof, to choosing a door, to enquiring. Optimise this path above all others.

Flow B: End client (partner-mediated, also serves direct)

  • Persona: Director Diane, the growth-stage SME or corporate marketing lead (highest value), and the time-poor tradie or local SME. Procurement Pat is the public-sector and not-for-profit overlay. All usually arrive through a partner.
  • Entry point: most often a direct link to Start sent by the partner who already holds the relationship; also local and vertical word of mouth, and direct or search arrival.
  • Key pages: Start, Work (to verify the proof), and How it works if the visitor wants the method first.
  • Conversion point: book or enquire for the $4,950 Growth Diagnostic on Start, credited toward the engagement.
  • Critical path: partner link to Start to book. The single most important thing to optimise is that a partner can send a client straight to Start and the page closes the booking with Pottsville one click away as proof. For the Procurement Pat overlay in not-for-profit and government contexts, a procurement and probity overlay slows this path and adds evidence needs; the page should make the accountable named human and the owned, documented outputs easy to point a procurement reviewer at.

6. Conversion Strategy

Two front doors at two commitment levels, deliberately not competing on the same button. The conversion funnel below maps each stage to the page that carries it and the CTA that moves the visitor on.

  • Primary conversion point: the partner enquiry on Contact, reached via the primary CTA carried through the nav and the close of For Partners. It is the main goal because the partner is the front door now.
  • Secondary conversion point: the Growth Diagnostic booking on Start, the end-client entry. Lower commitment, partner-mediated, but the engine of the pilot economics.
  • Tertiary, low-commitment actions: a general enquiry on Contact, and a showcase or founding-cohort interest expression. No newsletter signup in phase 1 (no content programme to feed it; the newsletter app at apps.maxxim.ai is a later capability, not a phase-1 conversion point).
  • Lead capture approach: short, single-step forms served from apps.maxxim.ai (the contact-form app; chat and newsletter apps come later). The partner enquiry form asks only name, contact, the operator's business or network, and which door interests them (license or refer), plus a free-text line. The Growth Diagnostic form asks name, contact, organisation, and whether a partner referred them (to preserve partner ownership of the relationship). Keep both short; a high-trust, founder-led sale does not need a long form, and length suppresses the partner enquiry most.
  • Trust signals placement: Pottsville, the gates, the named specialists, the named human, and the ownership guarantee sit immediately beside every conversion point, never more than a scroll away from a CTA. For the partner funnel, the founding-cohort and 3 July showcase moment adds genuine, unmanufactured urgency beside the For Partners CTA, and the live Jewell partner is the standing proof the model holds. For the procurement overlay, the named-accountable-human and documented, owned outputs are the trust signals a reviewer needs.
  • Exit intent and abandonment: no aggressive pop-ups, which would clash with the calm Sage tone. The recovery strategy is structural: a clear secondary CTA on every page so a visitor not ready for the primary action has a lower-commitment next step rather than a dead end. Every page ends in a CTA band; no dead ends.

7. Technical Requirements

  • Platform and stack. maxxim.ai is a live Astro site hosted on Cloudflare Pages, the same stack every Maxxim client site ships on, edited through the Maxxim engine and the dashboard at admin.maxxim.ai (Clent and Raef only). Images are served from assets.maxxim.ai and embedded apps from apps.maxxim.ai. The refresh stays on this stack; this is an edit-and-restructure, not a re-platform. Sites are locked (Astro) by default and can be unlocked to hand-editable HTML, which is a one-way door, so the refresh is planned and shipped before any unlock is considered. Re-platforming is out of scope and would burn the Deploy window for no strategic gain.

The embedded contact-form app at apps.maxxim.ai captures partner and Diagnostic enquiries, with partner-referral capture so relationship ownership is preserved; form-to-inbox routing or a lightweight CRM behind it; a calendar or booking link for the Growth Diagnostic and the partner discovery conversation; analytics (see Section 9); transactional email via Cloudflare email routing or Google Workspace for enquiry confirmations.
