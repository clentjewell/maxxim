Brand Guidelines

The brand book the rest of the engagement is measured against.

1 July 2026 · Prepared by Maxxim

Welcome, and thanks for the time. This is the Maxxim brand book, edition three, refreshed on the first of July. It is the one place we write the brand down: the strategy it stands on, the logo system, the exact colours and type, and how it all gets used in practice. Everything we build from here, the website, the copy, the partner hub, the third of July showcase, is measured against this. Two things changed since the twenty-seventh of June edition. There is now a reversed logo variant, documented and embedded in this book. And the product mechanics are now known and named, so Max, Sal, and Pip appear where they make the engine concrete. Philosophy

Built to look like a standard, not a startup shouting

  • The proven method, run by a human, at AI speed
  • Held together by a community, not a lone operator
  • Disciplined and high-contrast, never decorative
  • Trust earned through legibility and restraint
  • Finished, owned outcomes, not spectacle
The whole system serves one idea: the proven method, run by a human, at AI speed, held together by a community rather than a lone operator. Because we sell finished, owned outcomes and not spectacle, the look is calm and high-contrast. It should feel like a standard you would trust to sign off your work. That restraint is a strategic choice, not just taste, because over-claiming is exactly what the trust gap punishes. A Sage brand earns trust by refusing to dress plumbing up as the product.

The seam we make legible

  • The human twenty per cent and machine eighty per cent split, now proven by a live partner

The visual system is built to fit the strategy, so it helps to name it. We serve the Capacity-Capped Operator, the partner who can win the work and own the relationship but cannot deliver agency-grade growth repeatably, and through that partner we serve any organisation type at all. The core of the whole business is the split: the human holds the twenty per cent clients actually pay for, trust, taste, and judgement, and the machine runs the repeatable eighty per cent at speed. That split is now embodied by a live partner. Jewell enters and owns the client, the Discover phase is always human, and Design and Deploy hand off to the Maxxim engine at scale. Jewell is the name on the door. Maxxim is the engine behind it. The archetype is a weighted blend, Sage first for method-proven authority, Creator in support as the builder of an engine. The whole visual system exists to make that split obvious, credible, and ownable, and to keep the human the hero, not the machine.

New · Reversed (inverse) variant

White maxxim wordmark for dark grounds and Signal Blue fields. A variant within the locked identity, not a re-opening.

This is the new reversed, or inverse, variant, embedded here as the real vector file. It is the Edition 03 logotype knocked out in white, for dark grounds and Signal Blue fields. Use it wherever the primary Signal Blue mark would lose contrast: on solid Signal Blue panels, on Ink or near-black surfaces, and on dark photographic fields. Do not use it on white or a light field, where the white wordmark disappears; there we switch back to the primary Signal Blue mark, or to the single-colour mono Ink mark where colour is unavailable. It is white, full stop: do not recolour it, do not tint it, do not add a gradient, glow, or drop shadow, and never place any animal or mascot alongside it. It follows the same clear-space and minimum-size floors as the primary mark, with a little extra breathing room on a dark ground so the knockout does not crowd a panel edge. It is a variant, not a new identity. The identity is not re-opened by it.
Logo · Backgrounds and inverse rules

Match the mark to the ground

Primary Signal Blue mark

  • On white or a light field
  • On a white holding shape over busy or photographic detail
  • Where blue is unavailable, single-colour mono in Ink

Reversed white mark

  • On Signal Blue fields, panels, and bands
  • On Ink or any dark or near-black ground
  • On a Signal Blue holding shape over busy or dark photographic detail

On a dark photo, do not trust the photo to carry contrast. Use a solid Signal Blue or Ink holding shape behind the whole footprint. At the small end, prefer the white icon-only monogram over a shrunk wordmark.

The rule for choosing between the two marks is simple: match the mark to the ground. On white or a light field, the primary Signal Blue mark. On Signal Blue or a dark field, the reversed white mark. On a busy or photographic end-client field, place the mark on a holding shape rather than over busy detail: the reversed white mark on a Signal Blue holding shape, the primary Signal Blue mark on a white holding shape. Where colour is unavailable, the single-colour mono mark in the near-black Ink value, kept flat. On a dark photo especially, do not trust the image to carry the contrast; if any part of the clear-space zone would fall over a lighter passage, put a solid Signal Blue or Ink shape behind the whole footprint. And where a favicon or a very small mark sits on a dark UI, use the white icon-only monogram rather than shrinking the wordmark, so it stays crisp at the sixteen-pixel floor.
