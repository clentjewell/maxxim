1. Killer Questions

The truths that define Maxxim, distilled from the Discovery Dataset, the 29 June product guide, the live Jewell partner site, and the v2.1 strategic spine. If you read only this section, you understand the business.

  • Maxxim is the machine for an operator's judgement. AI runs the repeatable 80 percent of a marketing engagement at speed through three named specialists: Max does the words in Cowork, Sal does the pixels in the Design Studio, Pip runs the Social Studio. An accountable human partner owns the 20 percent clients actually pay for: trust, taste, judgement, and the relationship. AI proposes, the human decides and signs off. That split is the whole business.
  • Maxxim solves two problems with one engine. The partner problem: skilled operators have judgement without a machine, or tools without a method. The Jewell problem: direct delivery is capped by Clent's personal capacity. Maxxim is the structural unlock for both, and Jewell now runs live on it as the proof.
  • The partner is the customer, the client is the output. The trusted operator is the primary audience and the scale mechanism, reached through two doors: license to deliver, or refer for an optional finder's fee. Clients are commercialised through the partner. One named partner owns each client relationship, the single non-negotiable public rule.
  • The engine is built for any organisation, delivered through a human. The method reads across personal brands, solo operators, SMEs, corporates, not for profits, and all levels of government. The constant is the same everywhere: a proven gated method plus AI speed plus an accountable human. The variable is context, tier, and compliance.
  • Maxxim owns an empty quadrant: fast, accountable, and method-backed. No competitor category pairs AI speed with a proven gated method and a named human standing behind it. Agencies have the human without the speed, AI tools have the speed without the human, enablement platforms have reach without a method.
  • The method is proven and enforced by the product, not just promised. The 3D Process has run with real clients for years through Jewell, and the platform now enforces it mechanically: creative unlocks only as its briefs exist and a logo is locked. The gates are the moat. Protecting them is protecting the differentiator.
  • The central buying objection is trust, not price. "Can AI do this to a standard I will put my name on." The human-at-every-gate model is the structural answer, but it must be proven, not asserted. Cleared proof closes the gap faster than any other single action, and it is the standout vulnerability: the Maxxim brand has one cleared public case study of its own.
  • The clients own what they buy. A complete strategy plus launched assets the client owns outright, delivered in days not months, hosted on their own domain and unlockable to hand-editable HTML. A named human is accountable at each gate. Pottsville Acupuncture is the live proof of that promise.
  • The engagement compounds rather than ending at launch. The Deepen loop, modelled on the Pottsville pattern, is the recurring-revenue line agencies rarely systematise and tools do not offer. Extended across the partner community, that loop becomes a flywheel a lone operator can never build.
  • The community of partners is the durable moat. In an age where AI commoditises the first 80 percent, the advantage is the human layer held not by one operator but by a network of them: shared learning, shared deal flow, a shared quality bar. Ownership stays Clent and Raef, two owners, no board.

The core question, answered with a defended position

v2.1 asks what Maxxim is actually becoming: a product company, a partner network, a commercialisation engine, a software-assisted agency, or a platform for operator-led businesses. The Discovery evidence, now sharpened by the known product mechanics and a live partner in market, points to one defensible position: Maxxim is a commercialisation engine delivered as a platform for operator-led businesses. It is a licensable engine that lets trusted operators run a proven gated method under their own relationship, with Maxxim productising the method and Jewell owning the standard.

The reasons this position holds, and the readings it beats:

  • Not a generic software product. The value is not the tools. Only the thin layer (Max plus the engine) is built in-house; everything else is deliberately leaned on. What is sold is a method the tools serve, enforced by mechanical gating a tool vendor does not have.
  • Not an agency selling hours. Revenue is a platform floor plus a per-engagement clip scaled by lead source, not billable time. The partner, not Maxxim, carries the client relationship.
  • Not self-serve SaaS without judgement. A named human owns the 20 percent and signs off at every gate. Remove the human and the proposition collapses into the AI-tool category it is built to beat.
  • More than a partner network alone. The network is the scale mechanism and the moat, but the engine and the method are what the network licenses. Maxxim is the engine behind the partner, not only the room of partners.

This position was endorsed at Gate 1 with a wider aperture (the universal-audience decision above) and is carried, not reopened. See discover-addendum.html, Decision 1.

2. Audience Teardown (summary)

Two stacked audiences. Layer A is the partner side (primary), Layer B is the client served through the partner. The Gate 1 aperture is wider than SME: the engine reads across every organisation type, but it is always delivered through a partner. Full detail in audience-teardown.html.

  • A1 Capacity-Capped Operator (license door): a skilled operator with a network but no repeatable machine; pain is judgement without a machine; trigger is hitting the capacity wall, or seeing a peer operator (Jewell) run the full model in market. The primary audience and scale mechanism.
  • A2 Networked Referrer (refer door): a well-connected operator who will introduce clients for a finder's fee; pain is a network sitting idle; trigger is a contact who needs growth help plus a clean, documented fee arrangement.
  • A3 Scoped Specialist (specialist door): an expert who plugs into a defined module alongside Sal or Pip without owning the client; pain is scope creep and loose freelance positioning; pulled by live engagements as gates unlock.
  • B1 and B2 clients through partners: time-poor tradies and local SMEs, plus growth-stage and premium organisations; pain is the choice between slow expensive agencies and risky AI tools with no one accountable. They meet Max, Sal and Pip only through the partner, with an optional read-only portal and the assistant Ari.

Niche: trusted operators who want to deliver or refer agency-grade growth without building an agency, plus the organisations they commercialise. Primary persona: Operator Owen, the capacity-capped Client Partner, who signs at principal level and is motivated to scale without becoming an agency. Resolved at Gate 1: the partner is the front door now; an end-user self-serve portal is the north star, sequenced after the partner motion is proven.

3. Competitor Analysis (summary)

Competition is by category on two layers, not a single named rival. Full detail in competitor-analysis.html.

  • Small agencies and web developers (client layer): human expertise and a tangible deliverable, but slow, expensive, opaque, founder-capped, and no ongoing growth.
  • DIY plus general AI tools (client layer): cheapest and instant, but offload the work and judgement onto the owner and feel unaccountable, the AI-fatigue counter-trend Maxxim exploits.
  • Enablement and self-assembled toolchains (partner layer): reach and ready tooling, but no method and no quality bar, so output rests entirely on the operator.

Differentiators to lean into: a proven gated method enforced by the product rather than tooling; an assembled specialist engine (Max, Sal, Pip on best-tool-for-each-job models) rather than one tool; AI speed with a named human accountable at every gate; finished outcomes the client owns outright; a compounding Deepen loop. Competitive angle: own the empty quadrant, fast and accountable and method-backed, and make the human-at-every-gate the answer to the trust gap. Named threats: agencies adopting AI to move faster, and enablement platforms moving upmarket into method; the defence is the combination plus the enforced gates plus cleared proof, none of which a competitor can assemble quickly.
